One-Pan Dirty Spaghetti

Description of Recipe

This is my favorite method for making dirty spaghetti in one pan, which not only means fewer dishes, but the pasta absorbs more flavor than if boiled separately. Andouille, Worcestershire sauce, and lots of vegetables and seasonings make it really 'dirty', meaning really flavorful. Here, the term 'flavor bomb' is an understatement.

Ingredients

  • 2 Tbsp olive oil
  • 8 ounces ground beef
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 and 1/2 cups bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 cup celery, diced
  • 4 ounces Andouille sausage, diced
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/4 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/4 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 and 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 8 ounces spaghetti
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce

Steps

  1. Add oil to a deep skillet with a tight fitting lid and set over medium-high heat.
  2. Add ground beef, and cook for 3 or 4 minutes, breaking the meat up into small crumbles
  3. Once ground beef is browned, add onion, bell peppers, celery, and andouille sausage.
  4. Cook, stirring, for 2 more minutes.
  5. Season with onion powder, garlic powder, paprika, cumin, oregano, thyme, cayenne, black pepper, and salt, and cook, stirring, for 1 to 2 minutes more.
  6. Pour in broth and bring to a simmer.
  7. Break spaghetti in half and add to the pan. Stir.
  8. Cover the pan tightly. Cook on medium-high for 2 minutes.
  9. Uncover and give everything a stir.
  10. Repeat this process of uncovering, stirring, and covering every 2 minutes or so, until pasta is cooked.
  11. Turn off the heat and drizzle in Worcestershire sauce, plus another pinch of salt if needed. Stir to combine, and serve in bowls.
